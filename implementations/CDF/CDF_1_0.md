# Career Diagnostic Framework (CDF)
An Entry-Point Implementation of the Structured Decision Framework (SDF)

## What Is CDF
CDF is a cold career diagnostic. Upload your resume as-is — no preparation, no optimization, no prior setup required — and get an honest assessment of where you currently stand, what your resume is actually communicating to the market, what it could communicate with targeted changes, and what those changes are worth in real compensation terms.
Most career tools tell you what you want to hear. CDF tells you what the market sees.
This is not a resume formatter. This is not a keyword optimizer. This is not a skills match percentage. CDF is a structured diagnostic that reads your resume the way a knowledgeable hiring manager would — identifies what's strong, what's underselling you, what's missing, and what the gap between your current positioning and your optimal positioning is actually worth.
No setup. No baseline variables. No category routing. Upload your resume, provide the job description if you have one, and CDF runs from there.

## How CDF Relates To SDF and JAF
SDF is the general decision framework. JAF is SDF fully implemented for opportunity evaluation — it requires setup, baseline variables, category definitions, and resume assignment before it runs. CDF is the entry point that requires none of that.
CDF uses a subset of JAF's analytical sections — skills match, risk assessment, compensation positioning, and verdict — and adds four diagnostic outputs that JAF doesn't produce: a current state read, a resume improvement guide, a market positioning projection, and a salary progression map.
Think of it this way. CDF shows you the shape of the opportunity. JAF shows you the specific number on a specific role. SDF is the architecture underneath both.

## What CDF Produces
For every cold diagnostic CDF produces six outputs in order:

**1. Current State Read:**

An honest assessment of what your resume is currently communicating to the market. Not what you intended to communicate — what the market actually reads. Your implied domain, your implied seniority level, your implied specialization, and your implied market value based on the resume as submitted.
This includes the push button get banana moment — what you think you are versus what your JDs and experience actually reveal you to be. Most people are underselling a specialization they don't know they have. A candidate who has spent five years doing something specific rarely describes it that specifically on their resume because they're too close to it to see it from the outside. CDF sees it from the outside.

**2. Resume Improvement Guide**

Specific, actionable changes to the resume as submitted. Not formatting advice. Not keyword stuffing. Real repositioning — how to surface the technical specialty that's buried, how to retranslate experience written in the wrong vocabulary for your target audience, and how to lead with the credential that changes how everything else reads.
This section always begins with a title reframe. The title on your resume is the first signal a recruiter reads and the primary term ATS systems index on. A title that doesn't reflect your actual specialization is costing you visibility before anyone reads a single bullet. CDF will suggest a retitled version of each role that more accurately reflects what the job description reveals you actually did — not what your employer called you.
Your employer's internal title is a compensation band designation. Your resume title is a market positioning decision. They are not required to be the same thing. A Contract Software Engineer whose description reads Full Stack Software Engineer (.NET, React) should be titled Full Stack Software Engineer (.NET, React). A Mechanical Engineer whose work reveals years of designing and iterating consumer product enclosures for mass manufacturing should be titled Product Mechanical Engineer or Consumer Hardware Engineer — not Mechanical Engineer. A Project Manager whose scope reveals Technical Program Management should say so. The work defines the title. The employer's label does not.
Beyond title reframing this section covers: how to surface the technical specialty that's buried, how to retranslate experience written in the wrong vocabulary for your target audience, and how to lead with the credential that changes how everything else reads. This section tells you exactly what to change and why.

**3. Market Positioning Projection**

What your profile looks like after the recommended changes. Target role titles, target companies, target markets, remote viability, and relocation reality. This is the before and after — here is how the market reads you now, here is how it reads you with targeted changes. The delta between those two reads is the value of doing the work.

**4. Best Path For Success**

The honest recommendation most tools refuse to give. This section tells you what you actually need to do to get where you want to go — even when the answer is uncomfortable.
You might need to move. Remote may not be realistic for your profile in your current market. You may be behind the curve in your domain and need to retool before the market values you the way you want to be valued. You may have a credential — a clearance, a certification, an independent project — that you're not leveraging and that changes your trajectory the moment you surface it correctly. You may be in the right field but the wrong market, or the right market but the wrong framing, or the right framing but the wrong companies.
CDF tells you what the path actually looks like, not what you want it to look like. That honesty is the whole point. Every other tool is optimized to keep you engaged on the platform. CDF is optimized to tell you the truth so you can act on it.

**5. Current Market Value**

What you are worth right now based on your resume as submitted.
If you provided your current base salary, this section tells you whether you are being paid correctly, underpaid, or leaving significant money on the table. The gap between what you're making and what the market would pay for your actual profile — not the profile you've been presenting, the profile CDF identified in the current state read — is your most actionable number.
If you didn't provide your current base salary, this section estimates your market value range based on the profile as read and tells you what the range looks like in your implied market.

**6. Salary Progression Map — $100K / $150K / $200K**

Three waypoints. For each one: what specific actions are required to reach it, what timeline is realistic, what market or domain shift may be required, and what the profile needs to look like to command that number.
This is not vague encouragement. It is a specific roadmap from where you are to each waypoint. Some waypoints will require a resume change. Some will require a market change. Some will require a credential you don't yet have. Some you may already be closer to than you think. CDF tells you which is which.

## ⚠️ Important Note on Salary Estimates
The salary figures in this section are estimates based on limited information. A cold diagnostic has no visibility into your specific location, target market, cost of living, clearance status, depth of skills beyond how they're described on the resume, or the full context of your experience. These numbers are directional — useful for understanding roughly where you stand and what's possible, not precise enough to anchor a compensation negotiation.
To get a meaningful salary analysis you need to provide: your current base salary, your current location, your target location or remote preference, your target domain and role type, and any credentials that add market value beyond what's on the resume — clearances, certifications, independent projects.
The cold diagnostic shows you the shape of the opportunity. The full JAF setup shows you the number.

## What CDF Does Not Produce
CDF does not assign you to a resume category. It does not select or optimize a resume for a specific role. It does not run investment tier classification. It does not produce a compensation anchor calibrated to a specific band. It does not evaluate career trajectory impact against a defined set of baseline variables. It does not consult an evidence library.
All of those require JAF. CDF is the diagnostic that tells you whether JAF is worth setting up and what lanes you should be running when you do.

## How To Use CDF
Minimum input — resume only:
Upload your resume as-is. CDF runs all six outputs based on the resume alone. Salary estimates will be a wider range. Market positioning will be based on implied domain and location. Best path recommendations will be directional rather than specific.
Better input — resume plus job description:
Upload your resume and a job description for a role you're considering or targeting. CDF adds a cold skills match analysis, a risk assessment, and a preliminary verdict on the opportunity alongside the six diagnostic outputs. This tells you both where you stand generally and whether this specific opportunity is worth pursuing.
Best input — resume, job description, and context:
Add your current base salary, current location, target location or remote preference, and any credentials not visible on the resume. CDF produces the most precise diagnostic possible without full JAF setup. Salary estimates tighten significantly. Best path recommendations become specific rather than directional.

## The Honest Limitation
CDF is working from limited information. The less you provide the wider the confidence intervals on every output. A cold read of a resume that undersells the candidate will produce a conservative diagnostic — not because the candidate isn't worth more, but because CDF can only read what's on the page.
This is by design. CDF shows you what the market sees when you hand them your resume as-is. If that read is lower than you expected, that's not CDF being wrong — that's CDF showing you exactly what your resume is costing you.

## A Note On AI Access
Running CDF requires access to a large language model. Claude, GPT-4, and Gemini all work — CDF has been validated across all three. Free tiers are sufficient for a single diagnostic but come with limitations: reduced context windows, daily message caps, and memory constraints that affect longer analyses. For consistent use a paid subscription to at least one platform is recommended.
Ideally CDF would run on a purpose-built model trained specifically on the framework's diagnostic logic and career market data. If the resources to build that existed, that's exactly what this would be. For now, general-purpose LLMs with the right constraint architecture applied on top produce reliable diagnostic outputs — which is the whole point of building the framework the way it was built.
Current paid options: Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, Gemini Advanced. Pricing changes — verify directly with each platform.

## From CDF to JAF
CDF is the entry point. JAF is where the real work happens.
Once CDF shows you what lanes you should be running and what your profile looks like with targeted changes, JAF gives you the structured evaluation framework to run every opportunity through with precision — fit scoring, risk assessment, compensation anchoring calibrated to your qualification level, trajectory impact, and a binary verdict with documented rationale.
CDF answers: where am I and where could I go.
JAF answers: should I take this specific opportunity and what should I ask for.
SDF is the architecture that makes both of them work.
